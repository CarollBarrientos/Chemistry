Introduction

In this website we will be reviewing the “Red Cabbage pH Indicator Experiment” tested in our Chemistry
Lab, where a simple and natural method is used to analyze the acidity or basicity of various solutions:
purple cabbage juice as a pH indicator. This vegetable contains a substance called anthocyanin, a natural
pigment that changes color depending on the pH level of the environment it is in. Thanks to this
property, purple cabbage juice can act as a visual indicator, making it easy to observe how pH varies
among different substances. The objective of the experiment is to demonstrate these color changes and
relate them to the approximate pH value of each tested solution.

Hypothesis:

With each substance tested we might be able to see many different and colorful results due to their
difference in pH, having positive results in the experiment allowing us to understand and identify easily
which substances are acidic, neutral, or basic.
